# Business Context

## Company Background
This analysis is based on a real-world multi-channel consumer electronics business operating across several European markets.

The company sells products through:
- Direct-to-Consumer (D2C) webshop
- B2B wholesale partnerships
- Online marketplaces (e.g. Amazon, OTTO)

The product portfolio includes smart devices, accessories, and consumer electronics with different pricing and margin structures.

Company and product names are anonymized for confidentiality reasons.

---

## Business Challenge
The company operates in a highly competitive environment with increasing pressure on margins driven by:
- Rising logistics and fulfillment costs
- Strong price competition on online marketplaces
- Different pricing dynamics across sales channels

Management needs to ensure that pricing decisions improve profitability without negatively impacting revenue or long-term customer relationships.

---

## Analytical Objectives
The purpose of this analysis is to support management in:

- Monitoring revenue, profitability, and margins at an executive level
- Identifying high-performing and underperforming products
- Understanding how pricing changes impact revenue and net profit
- Evaluating pricing sensitivity across different sales channels
- Supporting data-driven pricing and portfolio decisions

---

## Business Impact
This analysis supports pricing and profitability decisions by:
- Highlighting the sensitivity of net profit to pricing changes
- Identifying channels with higher margin resilience
- Providing transparency into product-level contribution
- Enabling informed discussions between finance, sales, and management teams

---

## Why This Analysis Matters
Pricing is one of the most powerful levers for improving profitability.

This case demonstrates how relatively small pricing adjustments can have a disproportionate impact on net profit, underlining the importance of pricing analytics in modern B2B and multi-channel organizations.
